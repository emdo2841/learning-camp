About Learning Camp

The Learning Camp Initiative is a nonprofit organization committed to helping children in underserved communities acquire foundational literacy and numeracy skills. We implement the Teaching at the Right Level (TaRL) methodology and the Language Learning from Familiar to Formal (L2F2) approach to ensure that every child, regardless of background, can succeed in school.

Our Mission

To ensure that every child in Nigeria has access to quality foundational learning through inclusive, child-centered, and evidence-based approaches.

  • • Providing scholarships and educational support to underprivileged children
  • • Training and empowering teachers with modern educational techniques
  • • Building and renovating schools in underserved communities
  • • Promoting digital literacy and 21st-century skills

Our Vision

A Nigeria where every child can read and do basic math before leaving primary school.

Core Values

Equity in Education: Equal opportunities for all children
Community engagement: Collaborative partnerships
Sustainability: Long-term impact focus
Acountability: Taking responsibility for actions and outcomes
Child-Centered Teaching: Prioritizing each learner’s needs and interests
Innovative Teaching: Using creative strategies to enhance learning

Dukawuya Class Practice

Dukawuya Class Practice (Completed)A 3-week pilot program was implemented at Dukawuya Primary School, Kano, utilizing TaRL methods with over 200 students. Children were grouped by ability and received differentiated instruction tailored to their needs. By the end of the program, most students showed significant improvement in their literacy skills.

Our Approach

Teaching at the Right Level (TaRL): We assess students using simple tools and group them based on actual learning level—not age or grade. Instruction is tailored to each group’s needs, using engaging and playful methods.
Language Learning from Familiar to Formal (L2F2):We begin with the mother tongue to teach literacy before transitioning to English. This method builds comprehension by connecting learning to language that children understand.
